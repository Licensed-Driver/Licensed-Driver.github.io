About me

Hello there! My name is Layne, and I'm an undergraduate at the University of Alberta pursuing a Bachelor of Science Specializing in Computing Science. I want to use this degree and the knowledge that comes with it to become a software developer, but I am passionate about every field that involves computing science.

Currently, I am working on a game called ONUS, which can be found in more detail in a blog post, and creating low-level systems in C, such as 2D and 3D rendering software. I have always loved mathematics and sciences, which I leverage constantly to make sure that my programs incorporate the most efficient practices from all fields. I also just think a good math equation or function is super cool, so I use them whenever I see fit. Computing Science and programming have always been a hobby before a career, which means that I do what I do purely because I enjoy it, causing me to never give up where others might fail. Because of this, I taught myself how to code and continue to do so in every situation that I can, which allows me to solve practically any problem I encounter because I know how to find a solution without any guidance and how to make that solution the best that it can be. I look forward to working in the industry and seeing what challenges I can break through!
